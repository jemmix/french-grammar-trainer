PROMPT: Complete the sentence: "She ___ to the park with her dog yesterday."
RIGHT ANSWER: walked
EXPLANATION: "Walk" + -ed = "walked." Regular verbs form the past simple by adding -ed. "Yesterday" signals a completed past action.
WRONG ANSWER: walks
EXPLANATION: "Walks" is the third person singular present. For a finished past event, use "walked."
WRONG ANSWER: walking
EXPLANATION: "Walking" is the -ing form. Without an auxiliary like "was," it cannot stand alone as a past tense.
WRONG ANSWER: has walked
EXPLANATION: "Has walked" is the present perfect — used for recent or unfinished situations. With "yesterday" (a specific past time), use the simple past "walked."
