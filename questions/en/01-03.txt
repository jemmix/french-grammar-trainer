WRONG ANSWER: doesn't like
EXPLANATION: "Doesn't" is for the third person singular (he/she/it). With "I," the correct auxiliary is "don't."
WRONG ANSWER: don't likes
EXPLANATION: After "don't," always use the base form of the verb — no -s ending. "Don't like" is correct, not "don't likes."
WRONG ANSWER: not like
EXPLANATION: In English, you can't make a negative by putting "not" directly before the verb. You need the auxiliary "do" + "not": "I don't like."
