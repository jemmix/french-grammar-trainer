PROMPT: Which sentence correctly uses "while" with past continuous?
RIGHT ANSWER: While I was cooking, the phone rang.
EXPLANATION: "While" introduces the ongoing past continuous action "was cooking"; "rang" is the interrupting event.
WRONG ANSWER: While I cooked, the phone was ringing.
EXPLANATION: "While" typically introduces the continuous action, not the interrupting one.
WRONG ANSWER: While I was cooking, the phone was ringing.
EXPLANATION: Both actions continuous suggests simultaneous actions, not interruption.
WRONG ANSWER: While I cooked, the phone rang.
EXPLANATION: Both simple with "while" loses the continuous vs. momentary distinction.
