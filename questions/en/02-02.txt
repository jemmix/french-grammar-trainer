WRONG ANSWER: When a verb ends in -y after any letter, change -y to -ied: "play" → "plaid."

WRONG ANSWER: When a verb ends in two consonants, double the last one before -ed: "fast" → "fastted."
EXPLANATION: Consonant doubling only applies when the verb ends in a single consonant preceded by a single vowel. Consonant clusters (like -st) do not trigger doubling: "fast" → "fasted."
