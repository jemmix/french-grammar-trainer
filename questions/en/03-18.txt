ID: 03-18-001
TYPE: MCQ
PROMPT: Which sentence correctly shows two parallel actions in the past?
RIGHT ANSWER: He was cooking dinner while she was reading a book.
EXPLANATION: Both verbs in past continuous (was cooking, was reading) show two simultaneous ongoing actions.
WRONG ANSWER: He cooked dinner while she read a book. | Past simple for both suggests completed actions, not simultaneous ongoing actions.
WRONG ANSWER: He was cooking dinner while she read a book. | Mixing tenses doesn't show both actions as ongoing.
WRONG ANSWER: He cooked dinner while she was reading a book. | This shows one completed action and one ongoing, not two parallel ongoing actions.
