### 27. Word Order & Sentence Structure

1. Basic **SVO order**: subject + verb + object (She reads books.)
2. **SVOO**: subject + verb + indirect object + direct object (She gave him a present.)
3. **SVOC**: subject + verb + object + complement (They made her captain.)
4. **Adverb placement**: between subject and verb, after be (She always arrives early.)
5. **Adverb placement**: at the beginning or end of a sentence for emphasis
6. **Inversion** after negative adverbs (Never have I seen… Rarely does he…)
7. **Question word order**: auxiliary + subject + verb (Do you like…? Is she…?)
8. **Indirect question word order**: no inversion (I wonder where she is.)
9. **Fronting** for emphasis (This book I really liked. Beautiful, it was.)
10. **Cleft sentences**: It is/was… that/who… (It was John who called.)
11. **There is / there are** to introduce new information
12. **Position of adjectives**: before nouns (attributive) or after linking verbs (predicative)
13. **Position of preposition phrases** (I spoke to her on the phone yesterday.)
14. **End weight**: put longer/heavier elements last (She gave to the man sitting in the corner a package. → She gave a package to the man sitting in the corner.)
15. **Sentence connectors**: beginning, middle, end position
16. **Time, manner, place** order for adverbs/phrases (She sang beautifully at the concert last night.)
17. **Ellipsis**: omitting words to avoid repetition (I can swim and [I can] surf.)
18. **Parallel structure** in lists and comparisons (reading, writing, and speaking — NOT to read, writing, speak)
